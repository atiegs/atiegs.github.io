Chapters 6-8

Chapter 6

  1. What is term “edge cases” referring to in software development?
    2. When operating a program at its maximal or minimal parameters (hence the term “edge”), errors and bugs can occur which are often unforeseen or implausible.

  2. Summarize Linus Torvalds’ advice about “large projects” give in 2004
    3. His advice is to not undertake a large open source project, begin with a small project and go from there. By taking a small project, one can focus on the details and functionality without getting bogged down.

Chapter 7

  1. Briefly describe Hungarian notation.
    2. Hungarian notation is a method of naming variables designed by Charles Simonyi. Hungarian notation involves the use of prefixes to encode the data types of variables. A variation of Hungarian notation, Apps Hungarian, is popular amongst Microsoft programmers

  2. What does the author state is the “...single most challenging demand of software development?”
    3. The most challenging demand is creating a way to communicate between machines and programmers, programmers to other programmers, and programmers to users.

Chapter 8

  1. What does “eat your own dogfood” mean?
    2. Since those at OSAF were dog-enthusiasts, the term was redesigned in a reference to dogs, however, the same meaning applies. Much like a sous-chef tests the meals before sending them out to customers, so must software developers. Developers test their programs to see how the function before releasing them publicly.

  2. “When people ask for numbers that far out, the traditional thing that engineers do ....” When discussing the timeline for Chandler, how was the quote above completed?
    3. “When people ask for numbers that far out, the traditional thing that engineers do, is make them up."
