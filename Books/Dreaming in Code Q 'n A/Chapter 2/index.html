Chapter 2

  1. What was the lifetime as a supported product, of Lotus 123? When did Kapor walk away from it? Why did he walk away from it?
    2. Kapor resigned in 1986. The product had made him rich but not happy, so he decided to leave.

  2. What does it mean for a program to “fork?”
    3. A program forks when technical disagreements occur and different groups form to find solutions.

  3. Linus Torvalds used a “science” and “witchcraft” analogy referring to software, explain.
    4. Torvalds explains that, like witchcraft, software can be kept secret and hidden but will eventually die out unless shared. That is why we must take a scientific approach to software by sharing knowledge with the masses.

  4. Who, where, when demonstrated one of the first PIM software programs?
    5. In 1968, Douglass Engelbart presented the first PIM software program in San Francisco.

  5. People often refer to starting their computer as “booting” their computer. What was the origin of this term?
    6. It originated from the saying “pulling one’s self up by the bootstraps” and later created into a “bootstrap loader” program which would start computers.

  6. Where was the graphical user interface (GUI) developed?
    7. The Xerox’s Palo Alto Research Center developed GUI user interface.

  7. List three software project “train wrecks.”
    8. Vaporwave, Advanced Automation System (AAS), and Trilogy

  8. What software crisis? Scan this article, what does it suggest? http://www.drdobbs.com/architecture-and-design/the-non-existent-software-crisis-debunki/2401659108.
    9. The article addresses the “Chaos Report” claim that only 1/3 of software development teams are successful. In the article, the author debunks this claim.

  9. Scan down this article to the two conclusions (about eight paragraphs down). With two sentences, what is your take away from this? http://scribblethink.org/Work/Softestim/softestim.html
    10. Simply switching to an automated computer program to save a few bucks can have hazardous consequences. Small issues can occur within software making overreliance on it risky.
